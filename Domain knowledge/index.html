What drives attendance intent

Deploying capital

VCs, angels, protocol treasuries, corporate VCs — primary agenda is sourcing and closing deals. Time pressure is high. Brief, sharp pitches valued.

Raising capital

Founders pre-seed through Series B. Goal is meetings, not panels. Often at multiple events per quarter. Warm intros convert 3-4x better than cold outreach.

Building partnerships

Protocol BD, exchange BD, corporate Web3 teams. Looking for integrations, listing agreements, strategic MOUs. Often underestimated as match targets.

Regulatory intelligence

Regulators, policy advisors, legal/compliance leads. Not deploying capital but shaping the rules. High-value matches for compliance-first founders.

Market intelligence / press

Analysts, journalists, researchers. Secondary match value but critical for narrative building and ecosystem positioning.

The signal vs. noise problem

A 1,500-person crypto event generates ~1.1M possible pairings. Without curation, attendees default to social proximity (people they already know), panel prestige (whoever just spoke), or random hallway encounters. Structured matching replaces serendipity with intentionality — turning a conference into a capital deployment engine.

Web3 deal structure vocabulary
SAFE
Simple Agreement for Future Equity. Standard pre-seed/seed instrument. Converts at next priced round. Common in Web2-style Web3 companies.
SAFT
Simple Agreement for Future Tokens. Token equivalent of SAFE. Pre-sells future token allocation to accredited investors. Used heavily 2017-2022.
Token Warrant
Equity + token rights bundle. Investor takes equity via SAFE/priced round AND receives warrant for token allocation at TGE. Paradigm popularized this structure.
TGE
Token Generation Event. The public launch of a project's token. Timing and unlock schedules (vesting cliff, linear vesting) are major negotiation points with investors.
TVL
Total Value Locked. Key DeFi traction metric. The dollar value of assets deposited in a protocol. Like ARR for SaaS — primary signal of DeFi product-market fit.
RetroPGF
Retroactive Public Goods Funding. Optimism's grant mechanism — rewards past builders with token grants. Non-dilutive. Growing model across L2 ecosystems.
FDV
Fully Diluted Valuation. Token price × total supply. Investors care about FDV at TGE vs. entry valuation. High FDV = compressed upside. Critical for fund return math.
RWA
Real World Assets. Tokenized bonds, real estate, private credit, commodities. Fastest-growing segment in 2024-25. Attracts TradFi institutions and family offices.

Framework
Match Mechanics

Six dimensions that determine match quality. A strong match scores well on all six — but the weighting shifts based on context. Learn to read which dimensions matter most for each persona combination.

How dimensions interact
The knockout rule

Some dimensions are disqualifying regardless of scores elsewhere. A Family Office investor who requires equity can never match a DeFi-native founder who will only issue tokens — no amount of thesis alignment rescues that structural incompatibility. Always check structural fit before scoring other dimensions.

Hard knockouts: Token/equity mismatch · Stage outside investment mandate · Check size vs. round size incompatibility · Regulatory conflict (anon team + institutional investor)
Strategic value amplifiers

A Corporate VC at a 3/5 thesis alignment can still be a 5/5 match if the strategic synergy is exceptional. Coinbase Ventures in a self-custody wallet deal may underwrite at lower valuation *and* provide a distribution channel worth more than the check. When evaluating, ask: what does this investor unlock beyond capital?

Value amplifiers: Distribution access · Portfolio introductions · Regulatory relationships · Exchange listing leverage · Technical/BD team support
